Rice Paper Rolls

Rice Paper Rolls

Prep: 10 mins

Cook: 10 mins

Total: 20 mins

Servings: 12 rolls

Yield: 12 Rice Paper Rolls

Ingredients

  • Tofu/Tempeh:
  • 250 g firm tofu or tempeh
  • 3 Tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 Tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
  • Rice Paper Rolls:
  • 2 zucchini or zucchini noodles (300 grams)
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 cucumber
  • ¼ head red cabbage
  • 1 handful fresh herbs
  • Vermicelli (optional)
  • 12 rice paper (12 if going single layer or 24 if doubling up)

Instructions

  1. For Tofu/Tempeh:
  2. In a large flat-bottomed dish, whisk together the soy sauce, brown sugar, and sesame oil.
  3. Slice the firm tofu into slices and place in the marinade. Flip to coat both sides and let rest.
  4. Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F). Bake tofu for 30 minutes, turning once halfway through. Allow to cool.
  5. For Rice Paper Rolls:
  6. Prepare vegetables by slicing into long thin batons or using a noodle-making blade on your food processor. Arrange everything for easy access.
  7. Fill a large bowl or casserole dish with room temperature water.
  8. Soak rice paper in the water until softened. (I use two for each roll.)
  9. Lay softened rice paper smooth side down on your work surface. Add vegetables, tofu, and herbs in the first third of the paper with margins on either side.
  10. Fold the two sides towards the center, then roll tightly. Cover with a damp cloth to prevent drying out. Repeat for all rolls.
